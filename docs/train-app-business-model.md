# Railmate - Business Model Canvas

## Overview
A freemium mobile app providing premium train tracking and delay prediction services for European rail travelers.

---

## 1. Customer Segments

### Primary Segments

#### A. Business Travelers (35%)
- **Profile:** 25-55, frequent travelers, expense accounts
- **Pain points:** Missed connections, schedule disruptions, tight itineraries
- **Willingness to pay:** High (company pays or expense)
- **Lifetime value:** £200-500/year
- **Acquisition:** B2B partnerships, LinkedIn ads

#### B. Commuters (30%)
- **Profile:** Daily/regional train users, time-sensitive
- **Pain points:** Unreliable schedules, platform changes, delays
- **Willingness to pay:** Medium (personal expense)
- **Lifetime value:** £60-120/year
- **Acquisition:** App Store, word of mouth

#### C. Leisure Travelers (25%)
- **Profile:** Occasional travelers, planning trips
- **Pain points:** Unfamiliar routes, connection anxiety, language barriers
- **Willingness to pay:** Low-Medium
- **Lifetime value:** £20-60/year (seasonal)
- **Acquisition:** Travel blogs, social media

#### D. Rail Enthusiasts (10%)
- **Profile:** Trainspotters, data lovers, early adopters
- **Pain points:** Want detailed data, historical stats, tracking
- **Willingness to pay:** High (passionate)
- **Lifetime value:** £100-300/year
- **Acquisition:** Reddit, forums, Twitter

## 5. Revenue Streams

### Primary: Subscription (85% of revenue)

#### Pro Monthly
- **Price:** £9.99/month
- **Target:** Trial users, occasional travelers
- **Features:** All Pro features, cancel anytime

#### Pro Annual
- **Price:** £59.99/year (£5/month)
- **Target:** Committed users, best value
- **Features:** Same as monthly, 50% savings
- **Expected mix:** 70% of subscribers

#### Pro Lifetime
- **Price:** £299 one-time
- **Target:** Enthusiasts, long-term users
- **Features:** Permanent Pro access
- **Expected mix:** 5% of subscribers

#### Family Plan
- **Price:** £89.99/year (up to 5 family members)
- **Target:** Families, couples
- **Features:** Shared billing, individual accounts

### Secondary: API Licensing (10% of revenue)
- **B2B API access** - Sell data to travel companies
- **White-label** - Power other apps' tracking
- **Enterprise data** - Analytics for rail operators

### Tertiary: Partnerships (5% of revenue)
- **Referral commissions** - Booking partner referrals
- **Affiliate marketing** - Travel insurance, hotels
- **Sponsored content** - Destination promotions

## 10. Unit Economics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- **Organic users:** £0.50 (support costs only)
- **Paid users:** £4.00 (ads, marketing)
- **Blended CAC:** £2.00

### Lifetime Value (LTV)
- **Free users:** £0 (ad-supported future?)
- **Pro monthly:** £60 (6 month avg retention)
- **Pro annual:** £120 (2 year avg retention)
- **Blended LTV:** £90

### LTV:CAC Ratio
**45:1** - Excellent unit economics

### Payback Period
**2 months** for annual subscribers

### Monthly Revenue Per User (MRPU)
- **Free:** £0
- **Pro:** £5 (blended monthly/annual)
- **Overall:** £1.50 (including free tier)

- **Use:** MVP development, initial marketing
- **Valuation:** £3M pre-money

- **Timing:** Year 2, after product-market fit
- **Use:** Scale team, expand to new countries
- **Valuation:** £15-20M
- **Investors:** VCs (local/global)

### Series B: £15-20M (Optional)
- **Timing:** Year 4-5, if pursuing aggressive growth

- **Valuation:** £50-100M

### Exit Options
1. **Acquisition** - Apple, Google, Booking Holdings, Trainline

## Summary

Railmate's business model is built on:
1. **Freemium subscription** - Pro features for power users
2. **Low CAC** - Organic growth through design excellence
3. **High LTV** - Annual subscriptions, low churn
4. **Scalable infrastructure** - Margins improve with scale
5. **Multiple exit options** - Acquisition or IPO path

The model is designed to reach profitability by Year 3 with a path to £6M+ revenue by Year 5.
